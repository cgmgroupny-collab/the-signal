How to Bet with Bitcoin (Step by Step)

If you've never bet with crypto before, here's how to get started:

  1. Get Bitcoin: If you don't have BTC, buy it on a regulated exchange like Coinbase or Kraken.
  2. Create a crypto wallet: Transfer your BTC from the exchange to a personal wallet (Exodus, Trust Wallet, or a hardware wallet). This step isn't strictly required but adds security and privacy.
  3. Sign up at a sportsbook: Create an account at your chosen sportsbook. Crypto sportsbooks typically require less verification than traditional ones.
  4. Deposit: Navigate to the deposit page, select Bitcoin, and copy the deposit address. Send BTC from your wallet to this address. Most deposits confirm in 10-30 minutes (1 blockchain confirmation).
  5. Place your bet: Once your deposit is confirmed, navigate to your sport, select your bet, enter your stake, and confirm.
  6. Withdraw winnings: Go to the withdrawal page, enter your wallet address, and submit. Crypto withdrawals typically process in 5-60 minutes depending on the sportsbook.

Pro tip: Always double-check the deposit address before sending crypto. Blockchain transactions are irreversible — sending to the wrong address means permanent loss of funds.

Is Crypto Sports Betting Legal?

The legal landscape for crypto sports betting in the US is nuanced:

  • Licensed US sportsbooks (DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM): Legal in 39+ states, some accept crypto. Fully regulated and licensed.
  • Offshore crypto sportsbooks (Bovada, BetOnline, Cloudbet): Operate outside US jurisdiction. No federal law specifically prohibits individuals from placing bets on offshore sites, but these sites are not licensed by US regulators.
  • The gray area: The Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act (UIGEA) targets financial institutions that process gambling payments — not individual bettors. However, some states have stricter gambling laws than others.
Important: Verify the legality of online sports betting in your specific state before placing any bets. Laws vary significantly by jurisdiction. This article is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is crypto sports betting legal in the US?

The legality depends on your state and the sportsbook. Licensed US sportsbooks are legal in 39+ states. Offshore sportsbooks operate outside US jurisdiction — there are no federal laws specifically prohibiting individuals from placing bets on them, but your state may have stricter rules. Always verify local laws.

How fast are crypto withdrawals from sportsbooks?

Crypto withdrawals are significantly faster than traditional methods. Bitcoin: 10-60 minutes. Lightning Network: near-instant. Litecoin: under 30 minutes. Compare to bank wires (3-7 business days) or checks (7-14 business days). Jackbit had the fastest payouts in our testing at under 5 minutes.

What cryptocurrencies do sportsbooks accept?

Most crypto sportsbooks accept Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Litecoin (LTC), Bitcoin Cash (BCH), and USDT. Some like BetOnline and Cloudbet accept 20-30+ coins including Solana, Dogecoin, Cardano, and Ripple.

Are there bonuses for depositing with crypto?

Yes — crypto deposits typically get 25-100% larger bonuses than fiat. Bovada offers 75% crypto bonus (vs 50% fiat). BetOnline offers 100% crypto match up to $1,000. Cloudbet offers up to 5 BTC.

Do I need to report crypto sportsbook winnings on my taxes?

Yes. In the US, gambling winnings are taxable income regardless of payment method. Crypto betting adds complexity — you may also owe capital gains tax on crypto appreciation. Keep detailed records and consult a tax professional.
